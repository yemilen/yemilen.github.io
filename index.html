About

Hi, my name is Milen. I am a PhD candidate at the University of Amsterdam, in the CCI group. I am supervised by Prof. Tom van Engers, Dr. Giovanni Sileno, Prof. L. Thomas van Binsbergen, and Dr. Dannis van Vuurden.

Research

My research focuses on normative control for data access in healthcare research, particularly addressing the challenges of automating privacy regulations such as the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and Data Sharing Agreements (DSA) for compliant data sharing. My work bridges the gap between legal frameworks and their technical implementation, developing approaches including:

  • Purpose-based access control model derived from the GDPR's Purpose Limitation Principle (Article 5(1)(b))
  • Designing duty lifecycle management system for tracking and enforcing legal obligations (Articles 24-28 on controller and processor duties)
  • Translation of high-level social policies such as those found in data sharing agreements into enforceable access control rules using domain-specific languages like eFLINT

I've modeled the SIOPE DIPG/DMG data sharing agreement using eFLINT to enable fine-grained access control over rare disease clinical data.
