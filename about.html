About Me

Hi, my name is Milen. I am a PhD candidate at the University of Amsterdam, working with the CCI group. My supervisors are Prof. Tom van Engers, Prof. L. Thomas van Binsbergen, and Dr. Dannis van Vuurden.

Research

My research focuses on normative control for data access in healthcare research, particularly addressing the challenges of automating privacy regulations such as the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) for compliant data sharing. My work bridges the gap between legal frameworks and their technical implementation, developing approaches including:

  • Purpose-based access control model derived from the GDPR's Purpose Limitation Principle (Article 5(1)(b))
  • Duty lifecycle management system for tracking and enforcing legal obligations (Articles 24-28 on controller and processor duties)
  • Translation of high-level social policies such as those found in data sharing agreements into enforceable access control rules using domain-specific languages like eFLINT

In collaboration with healthcare institutions, I've modeled the SIOPE DIPG/DMG data sharing agreement using eFLINT to enable fine-grained access control over clinical data. The approach ensures regulatory compliance throughout the data lifecycle by automating manual tasks and minimizing human error.

Education

I hold a double MSc in Computer Science from Saarland University and University of Trento, where I studied Security and Privacy and minored in Innovation and Entrepreneurship through the EIT Digital program. I also studied technologies for active and healthy aging at the University of Trento for a year.

Before my PhD, I worked as a security analyst at XTN Cognitive Security. I completed my Bachelor's degree in Computer Science at Jimma University.
